About Nike

Home > About Nike

1. The Beginnings of Nike

Nike, founded in 1964 by Bill Bowerman and Phil Knight, started as Blue Ribbon Sports. It officially became Nike, Inc. in 1971. The company's early focus was on importing Japanese running shoes.

2. Innovation and Iconic Products

Nike is synonymous with innovation. The Air Max series, introduced in 1987, revolutionized sneaker design with visible air cushioning. The Air Jordan line, created in collaboration with Michael Jordan, is a cultural phenomenon.

3. Global Impact and Influence

Nike's impact extends globally. The "Just Do It" slogan inspires millions, and collaborations with athletes like Cristiano Ronaldo and LeBron James have shaped sports culture and fashion.

4. Social Responsibility

Nike is committed to social responsibility. Initiatives like the Equality Campaign promote diversity and inclusion. The Nike Foundation invests in community development and education.

5. Sustainability Efforts

Nike actively pursues sustainability goals. Using recycled materials in product design and reducing carbon emissions, the company aims to create a positive impact on the environment.
